About Me

Currently, I’m the Design Lead of OakNorth’s “Case Analysis” team, where we’re building data-driven tools for credit analysis and monitoring. My role is to define and validate the end-to-end experience of our customers and ensure the quality of the final software.

Over the past decade, I’ve been the first designer of several FinTech startups. As these were early-stage companies, with no product managers, zero design maturity and sometimes even no product, I had the extraordinary opportunity to fully own every aspect of the customer experience — a huge challenge that helped me immensely to grow as a generalist designer. It also allowed me to learn how to develop a strong design culture in an organisation, to help management make better decisions with a user-centred approach, as well as to grow and mentor design teams. By becoming a key player for product development, I’ve helped these companies achieve record-breaking results and raise multi-million funding rounds.

I am a self-taught and highly adaptable designer. Even after many years of career, I still like to get my hands dirty and approach every new problem with a beginner’s mindset, which makes me a great fit for agile and fast-paced product teams. I can work comfortably on every step of the design process, from research to prototyping, to carrying out user testing and producing detailed UIs. I also write functional requirements with product managers, work on metrics with marketing teams and collaborate on code with software engineers.

What gets me excited is working on challenging design problems: turning complexity into simplicity, building sophisticated products that are easy to use, enabling people to achieve more with less effort.
